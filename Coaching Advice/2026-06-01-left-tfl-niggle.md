# Left TFL Niggle — Assessment & Plan

**Logged:** 2026-06-01
**Status:** Monitoring — tolerated quality load (updated 2026-06-03)
**Region:** Left tensor fasciae latae (TFL), lateral hip

## Symptom report (athlete)
- Niggle in **left TFL**. Originated around the marathon **4 weeks ago**.
- Had been **improving**, then **resurfaced during the 2026-05-31 long run** (18.03 km steady).
- **2026-06-01 easy run** felt better but the niggle was **still present**.
- Athlete's hypothesis: onset coincided with the stride-length drop "around km 10" of the long run.

## What the data shows (500 m breakdown, long run)
- **Mechanical inflection is at km 8.5–9, not km 10.** Stride dropped 1373→1313 mm in one segment; vertical ratio bottomed (best) at km 8.25 (7.14%) and climbed thereafter. Athlete's "km 10" sense lagged the real shift by ~1 km.
- **Cadence locked at 166–168 spm the entire run** — never wavered. So *all* back-half pace loss came from **stride shortening**, none from cadence. This is the classic signature of a **fatiguing swing-phase / hip-flexor muscle** (TFL is one) — corroborates the story but is *also* the generic fatigue pattern, so not proof.
- **No gross compensation signature.** Stance time flat (248–254 ms) and vertical oscillation stable until the km-16 stop — no limp/guarding. Reads as a **low-grade load-tolerance niggle, not a gait-altering injury** (mildly reassuring).
- **Data limitation:** Apple Watch records **no left/right balance**, so a left-only asymmetry is **not directly observable**. Stride is bilateral — we can see *when* form shifted, not that the *left* side caused it.

## Interpretation
- Resurfaces only in the **back half of a long effort** and lingers on easy = **load-tolerance problem**, not a fresh acute injury. Tissue is fine at low load; threshold crossed under accumulated fatigue.
- Cadence is good (166+), so **overstriding is not the driver** → points to **hip/pelvic stability**: TFL classically overworks when **glute medius is under-recruited/fatigued** (common ~4 weeks post-marathon).

## Plan
- **Keep Friday rest.** Hold easy days genuinely easy (HR ≤128; 2026-06-01 at 123 was right).
- **Pull intensity** until it settles — do **not** chase the quality session while symptomatic.
- **Hip-abductor activation pre-run:** banded clamshells, side-lying abduction, lateral band walks.
- **Post-run:** gentle TFL / lateral-hip release.
- **Do not deliberately lengthen stride** — turnover is already where it should be.

## Red flags → see a physio
- Pain sharpens, or
- Migrates to lateral knee (ITB), or
- Becomes visible in gait (stance time would then show it).

## Objective marker to track run-to-run
- **Stride length & vertical ratio vs distance.** If, on future runs, stride/VR start degrading **earlier than km ~9**, the issue is getting more reactive → back off harder.
- Next step offered: overlay 2026-06-01 dynamics on 2026-05-31 to see if onset is creeping earlier.
